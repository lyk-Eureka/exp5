\begin{abstract}
This report investigates the performance of the Feed-forward Denoising Convolutional Neural Network (DnCNN) in image restoration. We conduct a comparative study against traditional optimization-based methods (ISTA, FISTA, ADMM) and the benchmark BM3D algorithm. Experimental results on the Set14 dataset demonstrate that DnCNN achieves the highest average PSNR (29.39 dB) and SSIM (0.8302), while significantly reducing inference time to 0.2779s per image. The results validate that deep learning-based residual learning captures image priors more effectively and efficiently than handcrafted iterative solvers. \\
\textbf{Source Code:} \textcolor{magenta}{\url{https://github.com/lyk-Eureka/exp5.git}}
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Image denoising is a fundamental research area in computer vision. While traditional solvers like ADMM rely on manual parameter tuning and iterative optimization, they often fail to generalize across diverse noise levels. DnCNN represents a significant leap forward by utilizing a deep residual architecture, effectively treating denoising as a supervised learning task.

\section{Methodology}
\subsection{Optimization-Based Methods}
ISTA, FISTA, and ADMM treat denoising as a minimization problem of a cost function containing a fidelity term and a regularization term. The performance is highly dependent on the choice of the regularization parameter $\lambda$, which requires empirical adjustment for different datasets.

\subsection{Deep Residual Learning (DnCNN)}
DnCNN (Denoising Convolutional Neural Network) employs residual learning to explicitly predict the noise component $\mathcal{R}(y) \approx y - x$. By leveraging 17-20 layers of convolutions paired with Batch Normalization, the model learns to identify noise patterns across multiple scales, ensuring high-frequency details remain intact.

\noindent \justifying
The data in Table 1 presents a compelling case for the transition from optimization-based to learning-based denoising. DnCNN achieves a PSNR of 29.39 dB, surpassing the highly regarded BM3D by 0.26 dB and outperforming ADMM by an extraordinary margin of over 40\% in relative fidelity. The SSIM score of 0.8302 indicates that DnCNN preserves structural integrity far better than iterative methods, which often introduce blurring. Furthermore, DnCNN is the most efficient algorithm, cutting execution time by nearly 50\% compared to BM3D. This efficiency stems from the model's ability to process images in a single GPU-accelerated forward pass, eliminating the need for the hundreds of iterations required by ISTA or FISTA.

\section{Conclusion}
This study demonstrates that DnCNN outperforms traditional denoising methods in every key performance indicator. By replacing handcrafted priors with deep residual mappings, we achieved superior noise suppression and edge preservation. The findings suggest that DnCNN's architecture is a robust foundation for more complex tasks such as blind denoising or image super-resolution, providing the necessary efficiency for deployment in real-world high-throughput systems.
